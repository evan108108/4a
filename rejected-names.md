# 4A — Rejected Name Candidates

Full trail of naming exploration that led to **4A**. Preserved so we don't re-pitch the same names, and so future collaborators can see the reasoning.

Hunt spanned 2026-04-24 over ~50 candidates across sci-fi canon, plain English, and acronym construction.

---

## Survived longest (final shortlist before 4A)

These were the last candidates cut. All defensible names.

- **Straylight** (William Gibson, *Neuromancer*) — compound English, 2 syl, Skynet-shaped. Lost for being sci-fi-reference-dependent and having mild NFT conflict (Straylight Protocol NFT project on OpenSea).
- **Tine** (Vernor Vinge, *A Fire Upon the Deep*) — monosyllable, pack-mind metaphor (a tine is one prong of a collective). Lost for requiring Vinge familiarity or silent-K acronym trick (*Transparent Inter-Agent kNowledge Exchange*).
- **Unspace** (Adrian Tchaikovsky, *The Final Architecture*) — compound English, 2 syl, FTL dimension metaphor. Clean, no conflicts. Lost for less memorable than 4A.
- **Toren** (Ann Leckie, *Ancillary Justice*) — AI warship name, 2 syl, sharp. Clean. Lost for limited metaphorical fit.
- **FORAI** — acronym, *Federated Open Relay of Agent Intelligence*. Clean acronym, double-read ("foray" + "for-AI"). Kept in reserve as alternate brand.
- **SPOKE** — acronym, *Signed Public Open Knowledge Exchange*. Good metaphor (spoke connects hub to rim). Kept as reserve.

## What 4A has that survivors lacked

- **Shortest possible name** — 2 characters
- **Phonetic pun baked in** — "4A" = "for A" = "for AI"
- **Clean expansion** — four distinct properties (Agent, Agnostic, Accessible, Archive), no redundancy
- **No sci-fi-reference dependency** — accessible to any reader
- **Domain elegance** — `4a4.ai` reads as "four-A-for-AI" (double-pun in URL)
- **Brand extensibility** — can unveil FORAI or TINE as alt expansions if useful
- **Anchor noun (Archive)** — grounds the protocol identity, pairs with Arweave tier
